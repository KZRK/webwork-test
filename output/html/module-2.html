Checkpoint 2.16.

The expression\(\frac{2^52^42^{-5}}{\sqrt{4^2}2^32^5}\)

equals \(2^n\) where \(n\) is:

Answer.

\(4\)

Solution.

\(\begin{aligned} \amp \phantom{\mathrel{{}={}}}\frac{2^{5}2^{4}2^{-5} }{ \sqrt{4^{2}}2^{3}2^{-5}} \\ \amp =\frac{2^{5+4-5} }{ ((2^2)^2)^{\frac{1}{2}}2^{3-5}} \\ \amp =\frac{2^{4} }{ 2^{2}2^{-2}} \\ \amp =2^{4 - (2 - 2) } \\ \amp =2^{4} \end{aligned}\)

Checkpoint 2.19.

The polynomial \(f(x)={\left(2-x\right)\!\left(x+3\right)\!\left(5x+3\right)^{2}}\) has degree , leading coefficient , and constant coefficient

Answer. 1

\(4\)

Answer. 2

\(-25\)

Answer. 3

\(54\)

Solution.

Since \(f(x)={\left(2-x\right)\!\left(x+3\right)\!\left(5x+3\right)^{2}}={\left(-x+2\right)\!\left(x+3\right)\!\left(5x+3\right)\!\left(5x+3\right)}\text{,}\) by multuiplying the highest degree terms in each factor we find the highest degree term to be \((-x)(x)(5x)(5x)=-25 x^4\text{,}\) and therefore the degree is \(4\) and the leading coefficient is \(-25\text{.}\) Similarly, by multiplying the constant terms from each factor of \({\left(-x+2\right)\!\left(x+3\right)\!\left(5x+3\right)\!\left(5x+3\right)}\text{,}\) we get the constant coefficient \((2)(3)(5)(3)=54\text{.}\) Therefore, \(f(x)=-25x^4+\cdots+54\text{,}\) where we have omitted the degree 1, 2, and 3 terms.
