Checkpoint 1.3.

Divide one fraction into the other:

\(\frac{7}{6}\div\left(-\frac{5}{4}\right)\)

If needed, use an improper fraction in your answer. Don’t use a mixed number.

Answer.

\(-{\textstyle\frac{14}{15}}\)

Solution.

When we do division with fractions, as with

\(\frac{7}{6}\div\left(-\frac{5}{4}\right)\)

we first change division to multiplication, and at the same time, flip the second fraction. In this case, we have

\(\frac{7}{6}\cdot\left(-\frac{4}{5}\right)\)

Then we do fraction multiplication as usual. The full process is:

\(\begin{aligned} \amp \phantom{\mathrel{{}={}}}\frac{7}{6} \div \left(-\frac{5}{4}\right) \\ \amp =\frac{7}{6} \cdot \left(-\frac{4}{5}\right) \amp \\ \end{aligned}\)

We can divide the first denominator and the second numerator by \(2\text{.}\)

\(\begin{aligned} \amp =\frac{7}{3 }\cdot\left(-\frac{2}{5}\right) \\ \amp = -\frac{14}{15} \end{aligned}\)

Checkpoint 1.4.

Subtract one fraction from the other:

\(\frac{9}{16}-\frac{15}{16}\)

When needed, use an improper fraction in your answer. Don’t use a mixed number.

Answer.

\(-{\textstyle\frac{3}{8}}\)

Solution.

To subtract two fractions with the same denominator, we simply subtract the numerators and keep the denominator unchanged.

\(\begin{aligned} \amp \phantom{\mathrel{{}={}}}\frac{9}{16} - \frac{15}{16} \\ \amp =\frac{9-15}{16} \\ \amp =\frac{-6}{16} \\ \end{aligned}\)

We can reduce this by dividing 2 into both the numerator and denominator.

\(\begin{aligned} \amp =-\frac{3}{8} \end{aligned}\)

Solution.

The brackets [ ] and parenthese ( ) have the same purpose. We usually use [ ] when ( ) are already used in the problem, so we can tell which pairs are which.

\(\begin{aligned} \amp \phantom{\mathrel{{}={}}} 8-2 [3-(6+5\cdot4)] \\ \amp =8-2 [3-(6+20)] \\ \amp =8-2 [3-26] \\ \amp =8-2 [-23] \\ \amp =8 + 46 \\ \amp ={54} \\ \end{aligned}\)
