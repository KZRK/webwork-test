What angle (in degrees) corresponds to -3 rotations around the unit circle?

-3 rotations is an angle of degrees.

Answer.

\(-1080\)

Solution.
SOLUTION If we go around -3 times, we make 3 full circles in the negative direction, which is \(-3\cdot 360^{\circ} = -1080^{\circ}\) .

Checkpoint 5.7.

A point \(P\) is often identified in mathematics using the notation \(P(x,y)\) to reference a point at coordinates \((x,y)\text{.}\)

If \(P(\frac{15}{16},y)\) is on the unit circle in quadrant IV, then \(y =\) .

Hint.

The equation of the unit circle is

\begin{equation*} x^2+y^2 = 1 \end{equation*}

If you are given x, you can carefully solve for y.

Watch your algebra!

Answer.

\(-0.347985272676876\)

Solution.

SOLUTION:

Evaluating \(x^2+y^2 = 1\) for \(x = \frac{15}{16}\) gives

\begin{equation*} {(\frac{15}{16}})^2+y^2 = 1 \end{equation*}

Solving for y yields

\begin{equation*} y^2 = {(0.347985272676876)}^2 \end{equation*}

Since the point is in quadrant IV, then y is negative and so \(y = -0.347985272676876\text{.}\)
