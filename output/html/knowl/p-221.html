This quadratic does not factor in an obvious way (because, at it will turn out, its roots are not rational). This time we should use the quadratic formula to find the roots:

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rcl} x = \frac{-b\pm\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a} \amp =\amp \frac{-(-6)\pm\sqrt{(-6)^2-4(1)(7)}}{2(1)}\\ \amp =\amp \frac{6\pm\sqrt{36-28}}{2}\\ \amp =\amp \frac{6\pm\sqrt{8}}{2}\\ \amp =\amp \frac{6\pm2\sqrt{2}}{2}\\ \amp =\amp 3\pm \sqrt{2}, \end{array} \end{equation*}

so the roots are \(3+\sqrt{2}\) and \(3-\sqrt{2}\text{.}\)
