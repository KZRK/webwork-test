Solution 3.1.

When we do division with fractions, as with

\(\frac{7}{6}\div\left(-\frac{5}{4}\right)\)

we first change division to multiplication, and at the same time, flip the second fraction. In this case, we have

\(\frac{7}{6}\cdot\left(-\frac{4}{5}\right)\)

Then we do fraction multiplication as usual. The full process is:

\(\begin{aligned} \amp \phantom{\mathrel{{}={}}}\frac{7}{6} \div \left(-\frac{5}{4}\right) \\ \amp =\frac{7}{6} \cdot \left(-\frac{4}{5}\right) \amp \\ \end{aligned}\)

We can divide the first denominator and the second numerator by \(2\text{.}\)

\(\begin{aligned} \amp =\frac{7}{3 }\cdot\left(-\frac{2}{5}\right) \\ \amp = -\frac{14}{15} \end{aligned}\)
