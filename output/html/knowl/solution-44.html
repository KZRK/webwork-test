SOLUTION:

Evaluating \(x^2+y^2 = 1\) for \(x = \frac{4}{5}\) gives

\begin{equation*} {(\frac{4}{5}})^2+y^2 = 1 \end{equation*}

Solving for y yields

\begin{equation*} y^2 = {(0.6)}^2 \end{equation*}

Since the point is in quadrant IV, then y is negative and so \(y = -0.6\text{.}\)
