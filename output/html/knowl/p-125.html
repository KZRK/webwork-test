More generally, if \(A\) and \(B\) are two sets of real numbers then their union, denoted \(A \cup B\text{,}\) is the set of all numbers that are in \(A\text{,}\) \(B\text{,}\) or both. Their intersection, denoted \(A \cap B\text{,}\) is the set of all numbers that are in both \(A\) and \(B\text{.}\)
