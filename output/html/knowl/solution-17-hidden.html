To subtract two fractions with the same denominator, we simply subtract the numerators and keep the denominator unchanged.

\(\displaystyle{\begin{aligned} \frac{1}{32} - \frac{13}{32} \amp = \frac{1 - 13}{32} \\ \amp = \frac{-12}{32} \amp \hbox{We can reduce this by dividing 4 into both the numerator and the denominator. }\\ \amp = -\frac{3}{8} \\ \end{aligned} }\)
