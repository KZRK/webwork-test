To subtract two fractions with the same denominator, we simply subtract the numerators and keep the denominator unchanged.

\(\begin{aligned} \amp \phantom{\mathrel{{}={}}}\frac{9}{16} - \frac{15}{16} \\ \amp =\frac{9-15}{16} \\ \amp =\frac{-6}{16} \\ \end{aligned}\)

We can reduce this by dividing 2 into both the numerator and denominator.

\(\begin{aligned} \amp =-\frac{3}{8} \end{aligned}\)
