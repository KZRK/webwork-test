Example 1.2.2. Cancelling Terms.

(a) Without using a calculator, show that \(\frac{4}{4+\pi}=\frac{1}{1+\frac{\pi}{4}}\text{.}\)

(b) Use a calculator to verify that \(\frac{4}{4+\pi}\neq 1 + \displaystyle\frac{1}{\frac{\pi}{4}}\) and that \(\frac{4}{4+\pi}\neq\frac{1}{1+\pi}\text{.}\)

(a) We can factor a \(4\) out of the denominator: \(\frac{4}{4+\pi}=\frac{4}{4\left(1+\frac{\pi}{4}\right)}=\frac{4}{4}\cdot\frac{1}{1+\frac{\pi}{4}} = \frac{1}{1+\frac{\pi}{4}}\text{.}\)

(b) \(\frac{4}{4+\pi}\approx 0.56\text{,}\) and \(1+\frac{1}{\frac{\pi}{4}}\approx2.27\text{,}\) and \(\frac{1}{1+\pi}\approx0.24\text{.}\)
