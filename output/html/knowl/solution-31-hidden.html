When we do division with fractions, as with

\(\displaystyle{\frac{5}{12} \div \left(-\frac{7}{8}\right) }\)

we first change division to multiplication, and at the same time, flip the second fraction. In this case, we have

\(\displaystyle{\frac{5}{12} \cdot \left(-\frac{8}{7}\right) }\)

Then we do fraction multiplication as usual. The full process is:

\(\displaystyle{\begin{aligned}[t] \frac{5}{12} \div \left(-\frac{7}{8}\right) \amp = \frac{5}{12} \cdot \left(-\frac{8}{7}\right) \amp \hbox{We can divide the first denominator and the second numerator by 4.}\\ \amp = \frac{5}{3 }\cdot\left(-\frac{2}{7}\right) \\ \amp = -\frac{10}{21} \end{aligned} }\)
